North Star Metric for SDLC Improvement

Phase 1 focus: Speed + Stability. Monthly/quarterly review; year-end improvement.

Phase 1 North Star

North Star (Phase 1)

Reliable Flow Velocity (RFV)
RFV = (# Features/Epics shipped to Prod) ÷ (Median Lead Time) × (1 − Escaped Defect Rate)
Why this is defensible: rewards shipping + faster flow, and penalises breaking prod.

Leading practices & flow signals you can steer early. Lagging production health tells the truth.

Health overlay (self → team → org)

Phase 1 health goal: improve fitness without injury.

Fitness score = (Steps completed) ÷ (Time) × (1 − Injury rate)
↳ Steps = shipped features
↳ Time = lead time
↳ Injuries = escaped defects/incidents

Don’t start with 47 biomarkers. Start with a simple, steering-friendly score.

Occam’s Razor: what we measure now vs later

Phase 1 (now)

  • RFV composite North Star
  • Leading Lead time (median), Throughput (# shipped)
  • Lagging Escaped defects (severity-defined)
  • Optional (pick one): Change Failure Rate or MTTR
Why: small set, hard to game, directly tied to SDLC mechanics.

Phase 2 (postpone)

  • Outcome events / adoption (true north later)
  • Predictability (say:do, plan accuracy)
  • AI spend / power users (avoid vanity)
  • Employee sentiment (keep as pulse, not target)
Health parallel: don’t chase VO₂max & body-fat scans while you’re still getting injured weekly.

Leading vs lagging (legend + governance guardrails)

Legend (minimal)

  • Leading DoR quality, WIP, cycle time, test trend, build health
  • Lagging escaped defects, MTTR/CFR, incident rate, customer pain
  • North Star RFV (composite) — use sub-metrics to drive action
Guardrails:
1) Fix “feature shipped” definition (size banding).
2) Pick one lead time definition and stick to it (Idea→Prod or Commit→Prod).
3) Severity threshold for escaped defects (so teams don’t relabel reality).

Health overlay

Leading: training consistency + technique checks (steerable)
Lagging: injuries + performance results (truth)
Guardrails: standardise what counts as a “workout,” “injury,” and “time-to-results.”

Without standard definitions, the metric becomes politics.
